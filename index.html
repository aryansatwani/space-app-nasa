Spreading Awareness - An Overview

about our challenge

The UAE is classified among the categories of countries with the highest rate of vulnerability to the potential impacts of climate change in the world. These changes could range from warmer weather, less precipitation, droughts, higher sea levels and more storms.

the climate today

The climate generally is hot and arid; yet, on the coast, humidity can reach over 90 percent in summer and autumn. Inland is far less humid although the temperature is higher; sometimes, exceeding 50 degrees Celsius before midday in July.

Living the situation

We, the Capri Suns, have published this website so we can all share our experiences on climate change.

Impact of climate change

Part one - On the sea level

The rising seas. In 2010, Stockholm Environment Institute's US Center completed a report for Abu Dhabi, addressing how climate change will affect ecosystems, infrastructure and the economy and what impact it might have on the health of residents. The Stockholm report found that the UAE could lose up to 6 percent of its populated and developed coastline by the end of the century because of rising sea levels.
